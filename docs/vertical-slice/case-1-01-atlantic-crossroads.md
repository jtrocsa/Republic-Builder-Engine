# Case 1.01 — The Atlantic Crossroads

## Purpose

The first playable Chronicle case proves the end-to-end loop:

1. Character creation
2. Chronicle Institute orientation
3. Codex issue
4. Map-table selection and time-travel transition
5. Compact historical exploration map
6. Field Mentor check-in
7. Evidence collection and dialogue
8. Exchange Ledger signature activity
9. AP-style MCQ and SAQ assessment mode
10. Codex submission and Institute archive return

## APUSH placement

- Unit 1 / Period 1: 1491–1607
- Focus: effects of Atlantic contact and the Columbian Exchange
- Central question: How did contact between Europe, Africa, and the Americas begin to reshape societies on both sides of the Atlantic?

## Target duration

35–45 minutes total.

## Content scope

Four playable locations:

- Coastal Landing
- Taíno Community Area
- Exchange Route Overlook
- Evidence Station

## Signature mechanic

**Exchange Ledger:** students connect a movement or item across the Atlantic, then explain origin, destination, affected groups, and historical consequence.

## Assessment

- 3–4 AP-style MCQs
- 1 structured SAQ with explicit A/B/C sections
- Sources remain open in a side-by-side assessment workspace
- Practice, assigned, and exam-simulation modes are separate states
