# Atlantic Crossroads — Source Notes

## Student-facing records used in the preview

1. **Context record:** Virgin Islands National Park timeline, AD 1200–1500. Used to establish that Taíno communities in the Greater Antilles had established societies before European contact, including agriculture, craft traditions, community life, and canoe travel.
2. **Primary source:** Christopher Columbus's 1493 letter reporting on the 1492 voyage. The preview uses a short excerpt and asks students to identify the account's European expedition perspective and royal audience.
3. **Visual-source note:** Library of Congress exhibit explanation that a 1494 printed edition employed woodcuts adapted from Mediterranean settings, demonstrating that historical images require sourcing before they are treated as depictions of an event or place.

## Source locations

- Library of Congress, “Columbus and the Taíno” exhibition.
- National Park Service, “St. John History Timeline.”
- College Board AP U.S. History Course and Exam Description, Unit 1 / Period 1 framework.

## Editorial principle

The preview does not treat a European written account as a transparent account of Indigenous life. It pairs the account with contextual evidence and makes source perspective explicit.
